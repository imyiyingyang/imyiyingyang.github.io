My Practicum at Multicutural Student Affairs Office

University of Miami

Introduction of MSA Office

The Office of Multicultural Student Affairs provides services and resources that support the academic mission of the institution. They provide a supportive environment where individual growth and cultural expression is encouraged and academic excellence expected. Each of student has embarked on a journey to become better educated citizens.

The mission of the Office of Multicultural Student Affairs (MSA) is to provide leadership and advocacy for underrepresented and ethnically diverse students at the University of Miami. The office provides direction in developing programs and services that enhance the retention and successful matriculation of students, while supporting their personal and academic development. In addition, MSA supports the university in its ongoing efforts to maintain a multicultural campus community, which supports, values, and celebrates its diversity.

My Learning Objectives

Objective 1: To gain an understanding of the roles and functions of the positions within the Office of Multicultural Student Affairs.

Objective 2: To participate in programming and services available to University of Miami students through MSA to create a more diverse and inclusive community.

Objective 3: To benchmark diversity programs offered by other institutions.

Objective 4: To assist and advice Unity Roundtable and Asian American Student Association with programming and related activities.

Activities

  • Attend and participate in weekly staff meetings.
  • Work with Asian American Students Association and Unity Roundtable; attend their meetings to know their ideas, report their needs and suggestions to the MSA Office, and to help students get involved in campus life.
  • Attend and assist with other programs and activities held by MSA Office, attend meetings in other affiliated student organizations if available.
  • Evaluate the programs and events held by other institutions, learn their strengths, and examine the possibility to develop special initiatives at UM.
  • Apply theory to practice, analyze student and staff behaviors to determine which theories are applicable. For example, student development theory, student diversity theory, or challenges for student affairs practitioner.

Skills and Compentencies

  • Exhibit active listening skills (appropriately establish interpersonal contact, paraphrasing, summarizing, questioning, and clarifying).
